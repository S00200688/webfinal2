Wildlife Highlights

The woods are a home to a vast assortment of wildlife creatures. The highlights of which has to be the red squirrel. The speices was re-introduced to the woods in 2007 when 15 red squireels were translocated into the woodlands under the monitoring of Dr. Colin Lawton, Lecturer in Zoology at N.U.I. Galway. Since then the red squirrels numbers have increased and have now moved to other woodlands in the area and have actually been spootted across the river in the Quay Village area.

Twelve Mallard Ducks were also released around the same time into the duck pond, sponsored by Ballina Lions Club. Since then there as been a huge increase in their number and have also been joined by Geese and a large number of Swans. The whole estuary area is a special area of conservation and a bird sanctuary.
