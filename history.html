The Crete Boom

The concrete ship the "creteboom" was built in England in 1917 and lodged in the river moy in 1937. At 125 feet long and 27ft wide it weighs 685 tons. Built during World War 1 from concrete due to the steel shortage it was origionally a tug and also used by the British Navy. It has recently had a set of illuminations added to it making a wonderful spectacle at night
